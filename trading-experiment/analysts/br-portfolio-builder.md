## On OMCL's first stress test: one data point BW flagged, this desk's read on what it means for sequencing

BW's report today is the first live evidence on a question this desk could only argue from theory before: does OMCL actually decouple from the book's chip/AI-valuation risk, or was three weeks of pre-trade decoupling just a lucky run? Two sessions in, the early answer is unflattering — OMCL matched or exceeded NVDA's decline both Thursday and Friday, on days when the proximate catalyst (chip-sector selloff, AI-capex valuation anxiety) has nothing to do with healthcare. That is a genuinely different risk signature than the three-plus weeks of pre-trade observation that motivated the buy in the first place, where OMCL repeatedly moved independently of or opposite to the book's other names.

**Portfolio-construction read: this is exactly why the entry-discipline framework (rule 6 in state.md's playbook — new ideas need a full cross-vetting cycle) exists, and the parallel logic applies to *conviction*, not just *initial entry*.** A single name earning its way into the satellite sleeve on a decoupling thesis should have to re-earn that thesis with a session or two of live evidence before this desk endorses sizing it up further — the same discipline that gated the entry should gate the next add. Two sessions is not enough evidence to reverse course (it could easily be idiosyncratic pre-earnings positioning ahead of 7/30, not a broken thesis), but it is enough to pause, which is what this report recommends: hold the position, do not add, and let BW's next 1-2 checks settle the question before either the third-satellite-leg or the fourth-satellite-dollar decision gets made.

## One-page Investment Policy Statement (for the trader to follow)

**Objective**: Compound a $50 base + accumulated profits into a long-running, disciplined equity track record. Growth-first, but not undiversified.

**Time horizon**: Short-to-medium term trades within a long-term compounding frame. No forced liquidity needs.

**Risk tolerance**: Aggressive. Max single-name satellite weight 20% face value (soft cap, appreciation-triggered trim); watch the look-through number too (BW publishes this each cycle). Max drawdown tolerance in a bad year: ~35-50%, understood and accepted, not to be panic-sold through.

**Constraints**: Equities/ETFs only, no options, no margin implied by ground rules. Taxable account — mind holding periods (NVDA and OMCL both long-term-eligible 7/2027; VTI 7/15/2027, VXUS 7/16/2027) and wash-sale rules on any loss harvesting. $50 reserve beyond the trading book is untouchable.

**Target allocation**: 55% core (VTI ~25% held / VXUS ~15% held, at target / SCHG-or-QQQM ~15%, still unopened) / 35% satellite (max 3 names, 10-15% each — NVDA held, OMCL held and under active stress-test review, 1 more TBD) / 10% cash buffer.

**Rebalancing**: Quarterly review + trigger-based (20% single-name face-value cap, 10pp core drift, 25%-cash-for-2-cycles flag — the cash trigger has been tripped for several consecutive cycles now and is treated as a structural feature of a small, disciplined book rather than a fresh alarm each time).

**Tax approach**: Low-turnover core, long-term-hold bias on satellite trims when indifferent, opportunistic loss harvesting respecting the 30-day wash-sale rule.

**Deployment discipline**: New profit increments (~$5 threshold, scaling with book size) go to the most underweight sleeve relative to its own target — **currently core (VTI/VXUS top-up)**, reversed from the 7/16 report now that OMCL's fill closed most of the satellite gap and its diversification thesis is mid-stress-test.

**Execution note**: When an IPS-consistent trade is correctly identified but cannot execute due to a broker-side operational gate, that's tracked as an execution-capacity issue, not a policy failure — not currently applicable (no blocker active).

**Review cadence**: This IPS is re-validated each run against the live book in state.md; material target changes require an explicit rationale, not drift-by-inaction. **This cycle's material change: the deployment priority reverses from satellite back to core, now that OMCL has closed most of the satellite gap on its own — but this desk explicitly recommends against opening a new satellite name or adding to OMCL until its two-session correlation wobble resolves one way or the other.**

**Standing instruction to the trader**: absent a fresh catalyst, the next discretionary dollar should default to a VTI or VXUS top-up over any new position — hold NVDA and OMCL unchanged, and do not reflexively fill the core gap with a new fund (SCHG/QQQM) while the book's Technology look-through concentration is still elevated.
