BW's 7/15 report is the first time this desk has had hard, sourced numbers (Vanguard's own 5/31/26 factsheet) rather than a rough estimate: VTI's Technology weight is 42.30%, not the 36.95% used in every report through 7/14. Re-running the math on the current sleeve (NVDA $5.245 direct + VTI $9.976, of which ~$4.22 is embedded Technology and ~$0.67 is embedded NVDA): **combined Technology look-through is ~62.1% of deployed capital, combined NVDA look-through is ~38.7%.** Two portfolio-construction conclusions follow directly:

1. **The core sleeve's first three candidate tickers (VTI/SCHG/QQQM) are all US large-cap/tech-tilted by construction.** Filling the rest of the 55% target with more of the same three names would raise the core-sleeve completion percentage while making BW's concentration numbers worse, not better — a classic case of optimizing the wrong metric. VXUS breaks this pattern: it adds core-sleeve diversification (asset class + geography) without adding to the same sector concentration.
2. **The satellite sleeve's job is now explicitly to be the sector diversifier, not just a "third position."** OMCL (healthcare) and, eventually, a vetted CRWD (software/security, still tech-adjacent but SaaS-model rather than semiconductor/AI-compute) are the correct candidates precisely because they sit outside the AI-compute/semiconductor cluster that VTI, QQQM, SCHG, and NVDA all share exposure to.
