## 1. Exelixis (EXEL) — best estimate **~July 27-28, 2026** — ~6-7 days out — **date NOT company-confirmed**

### Date-uncertainty flag (read this first)
This desk has now tried to source a company-confirmed Q2 2026 date across two consecutive cycles and still cannot find an Exelixis press release naming one (search results repeatedly cross-contaminate with ExlService Holdings/EXLS and Exelon, unrelated tickers with similar names). Two independent estimate paths converge on **late July**: (a) Q2 2025 was reported **July 28, 2025**, and earnings dates for this name have historically repeated the same week each year; (b) Exelixis's own recent cadence — Q4 2025 on Feb 10, Q1 2026 on May 5 — is a clean ~13-week rhythm that projects to **~July 28, 2026**. Against that, one aggregator (MarketChameleon-sourced) returned an unconfirmed **August 5, 2026** estimate this cycle — suspiciously identical to OXY's confirmed date, and this desk suspects cross-ticker contamination rather than a real data point, but cannot rule it out. **Net: treat July 27-28 as the working date, but confirm via a same-week options-chain or IR press-release check before assuming the window has passed.**

### Last 4 quarters vs. estimates
| Quarter | EPS actual vs. est. | Revenue actual vs. est. | Stock reaction |
|---|---|---|---|
| Q2 2025 (Jul 28, 2025) | $0.75 vs. $0.64 est. (+17.2% beat) | $568M vs. $578.46M est. (-1.8%, slight miss) | **+0.86%** (after-hours) |
| Q3 2025 (Nov 4, 2025) | $0.78 vs. $0.685-0.69 est. (+13.0% beat) | $598M vs. $590.47M est. (+1.3% beat) | **-0.5%** close, **-2.05%** after-hours |
| Q4 2025 (Feb 10, 2026) | $0.97 vs. $0.80 est. (+21.25% beat) | $598.66M vs. $604.56M est. (-1.0%, slight miss) | **-2.21%** after-hours (revenue miss overshadowed the EPS beat) |
| Q1 2026 (May 5, 2026) | $0.87 vs. $0.75-0.77 est. (+13-17% beat) | $611M vs. $608.95M est. (+0.3% beat) | **+9.0%** (clean beat-and-raise, plus a new buyback) |

**Pattern: EPS has beaten 4 for 4, but the stock reaction has been genuinely mixed — two beats sold off (Q2'25 revenue miss was minor and still got a tepid +0.86%; Q3'25 and Q4'25 both fell despite EPS beats, on revenue softness or margin nuance), while the one quarter with a clean beat on both lines plus a new buyback (Q1'26) popped +9%.** This is closer to GEHC's "beating isn't enough" pattern than to OMCL's "beats reliably pop" pattern — the market is parsing revenue quality and capital-return signals, not just the EPS headline.

### Q2 2026 consensus for the upcoming print
- **EPS: ~$0.85-0.86** (Zacks consensus, +14.7% YoY implied)
- **Revenue: ~$635M** (+11.7% YoY implied)
- Zacks Earnings ESP reads +6.31% (Most Accurate Estimate above the Zacks Consensus), a soft signal favoring another beat — but per the pattern above, a beat alone hasn't been sufficient for a positive reaction in 2 of the last 4 prints.

### Key metrics to watch
- **CABOMETYX growth rate** — Q1 2026 showed 8% YoY growth and the highest-ever quarterly new patient starts; whether that pace holds is the core commercial read.
- **Revenue vs. EPS quality** — given the pattern above, a Q2 print that beats EPS but misses or barely meets revenue (as in Q3'25/Q4'25) is a real risk of a negative reaction despite a "beat" headline.
- **Zanzalintinib regulatory progress** — FDA review ongoing for metastatic colorectal cancer, **PDUFA target action date December 3, 2026**. Any commentary on approval odds, launch prep, or a CRL risk is a structural (not just quarterly) catalyst; still ~4+ months out but increasingly relevant to sentiment.
- **Capital return commentary** — Q1's new buyback authorization was a specific driver of the +9% reaction; watch whether management reiterates pace/size.

### Segment/product revenue breakdown
Single-franchise business, not multi-segment: **cabozantinib franchise (predominantly CABOMETYX)** was $555M of Q1 2026's $611M total (~91%), with CABOMETYX itself at $552.8M. The non-cabozantinib remainder is early pipeline/licensing, immaterial to the quarterly headline. No structural segment-mix trend to track here the way GEHC or PTCT have — this print is almost entirely a CABOMETYX growth-rate story.

### Management guidance from last call (May 5, 2026)
FY2026 guidance reaffirmed: **total revenues $2.525-2.625B, net product revenues $2.325-2.425B** — guidance explicitly **excludes** any zanzalintinib approval/launch revenue, so a positive PDUFA outcome later this year would be incremental upside not yet baked into the number.

### Options market implied move

One data point (31.89% IV, "expected move of 0.72 (1.55%) with 9 days to expiration") appears to reference a near-dated weekly option, not specifically the earnings-week contract, so this desk is not treating it as a reliable earnings-implied-move proxy. A second source cited 55% IV generically. **Recommend a dedicated options-chain pull in the final 2-3 trading days before the print**, same caveat pattern as OMCL/GEHC/PTCT in prior cycles.

### Historical stock price reaction after last 4 reports
Q1'26 **+9.0%** | Q4'25 **-2.21%** | Q3'25 **-0.5% to -2.05%** | Q2'25 **+0.86%**. Only one of the last four moved more than 3% in either direction — meaningfully lower-variance than OMCL or GEHC, but the direction has been roughly split, not a clean pattern either way.

### Bull case
A clean beat on both EPS and revenue (unlike the Q3'25/Q4'25 pattern), continued CABOMETYX new-patient-start momentum, and any incremental positive zanzalintinib/PDUFA commentary repeats the Q1'26 playbook. Price impact estimate: **+7% to +10%**.

### Bear case
A repeat of the Q3'25/Q4'25 pattern — EPS beat, revenue in-line-to-soft — reads as deceleration in the core CABOMETYX growth story and draws a sell-off despite the headline beat. Downside risk estimate: **-3% to -6%**, anchored on the two most recent "beat but sold off" prints (this name's moves have been smaller in both directions than OMCL/GEHC historically).
