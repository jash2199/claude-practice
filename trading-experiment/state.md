## Strategy & theories (curated playbook — rewritten in the 2026-07-17 weekly distillation; full run-by-run reasoning stays in git history)

**Core, proven rules (keep applying):**
1. **No fresh catalyst, no trade.** Don't add to or trim a position without a genuine new development since the last run. A quietly-sitting gain/loss is not itself a reason to act. This is the single biggest reason "many runs correctly result in no trade."
2. **Don't chase an extended move.** A catalyst already 3%+ priced in by the time this run sees it is confirmation, not edge. Prefer pullback/consolidation entries over buying mid-spike.
3. **Geopolitical/macro-shock veto (single-name only).** Don't open a new single-name idiosyncratic position (OMCL, OXY, MU, etc.) during an active, still-unfolding shock (war, closed shipping chokepoint, oil spike, broad futures gap-down). Diversified basket buys (VTI, VXUS, and by extension any broad ETF) are exempt — their price already reflects the market's live view of the crisis, so buying one isn't a bet on how the crisis resolves. Validated twice (VTI 7/15, VXUS 7/16) with no downside surprises traceable to this reasoning.
4. **Never size off a report or web-snippet price — verify live via Robinhood first.** Caught real errors this way: GS's MU price was off by ~$250 (7/13, GS self-corrected once MS's DCF flagged the discrepancy); a 7/17 WebSearch on OMCL returned a stale $43.01 figure while the live Robinhood quote was $46.64. Analyst reports and search snippets are directional research, not an execution price source.
5. **A DCF "sharply overvalued" call is a hard pass, full stop** — regardless of how good a screener's momentum/technical case looks (MU, ~71% DCF downside, never traded despite being GS's #1 idea for a stretch). Conversely, a DCF that *agrees* with a screener pick (OMCL, MS ~18-25% upside) is a strong green light, not proof by itself — still needs its own entry discipline.
6. **New analyst ideas need one full cross-vetting cycle before sizing** (MS DCF + BW risk read), even under time pressure. Applied consistently to OXY, CRWD, PTCT, EXEL — none traded, no missed opportunity cost has materialized so far.
7. **When a good thesis is blocked by a timing veto, set a falsifiable trigger with a hard time-box up front — then honor it, including when analyst consensus builds and creates social pressure to jump early.** This is the single most validated process lesson of the book's first two weeks. Set 2026-07-13 for OMCL (three conditions: ceasefire holding a session, Brent <~$75, or a hard Friday 7/17 time-box with a pre-committed half-size fallback). Held the line for 5 straight trading days despite the strongest cross-analyst consensus this book has seen (GS #1 + MS's first-ever agreeing DCF + JPM clean earnings read + BW/BR endorsement) — then the time-box fired exactly as designed on 7/17 and the pre-committed $5 half-size OMCL entry executed cleanly, on a day OMCL happened to be pulling back (good price) and decoupled from a fresh chip-sector/AI-valuation selloff hitting the rest of the book. Reuse this pattern (trigger + time-box + pre-committed fallback size) for any future thesis blocked by a timing concern instead of open-ended "wait and see."
8. **Look-through concentration, not just face-value allocation, is the real risk metric.** VTI/VXUS's own sector/single-stock weights (VTI ~42% Technology, ~6-7% NVDA; VXUS carries real semiconductor-supply-chain names like TSMC/Samsung/SK Hynix) mean a "core sleeve" buy dilutes concentration by less than the headline % suggests. 9. **Distinguish the risk factors a candidate is actually diversifying against.** OMCL (healthcare) is uncorrelated with both the Iran/oil shock AND the separate AI-capex/chip-valuation rotation that hit the book hard the week of 7/13-7/17 — worth remembering it's a two-birds diversifier, not just an anti-geopolitical-shock hedge.
